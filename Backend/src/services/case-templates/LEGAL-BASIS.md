# Case-Template Legal Basis

This document records the legal authorities — statutes, case law, and Pakistani drafting authorities — behind each plaint template generated by `src/scripts/generateCaseTemplates.js`. Drop this directly into the FYP report appendix as the "Domain Study" reference for Module 3 (Online Document Editing and Digital Case File PDF Preparation).

All ten templates listed in the `case_types` table in [schema.sql](../../models/schema.sql) are now filled in. The first four were derived from the consulting advocate's reference plaints (Khula, Maintenance, Dowry, Declaration); the remaining six were drafted directly from the canonical Pakistani drafting forms (Mulla on CPC, Aamer Raza Khan, Tanzil-ur-Rahman) and cross-checked against the statutes they cite.

Regenerate any template after edits by running:

```bash
npm run generate:case-templates
```

## What every template contains

Each `.docx` has the same section structure, marked internally as **Heading 1** so Word's Navigation Pane (and our future Tiptap editor's sidebar via mammoth → `<h1>`) shows them. Headings are styled small / italic / gray so they read as section dividers rather than headline-style competition with body text.

| Heading 1 (visible in sidebar) | What it contains |
|---|---|
| Cause Title & Parties | Court name, suit number, plaintiff + defendant blocks, subject line. *CPC Order VII Rule 1(a)–(c).* |
| Body of the Plaint | Numbered "That..." paragraphs of fact + cause of action + jurisdiction + court-fee paragraphs. *CPC Order VII Rule 1(d)–(f).* |
| Prayer & Signature | `PRAYER` block with reliefs sought + signature placeholders. *CPC Order VII Rule 1(g).* |
| Verification | Oath under *CPC Order VI Rule 15*. Separate page. |
| Schedule of Witnesses *(family only)* | Mandated by *Family Courts Act 1964 §7(2)*. |
| Schedule of Documents Produced *(family only)* | Annexure table. Mandated by *FCA 1964 §7(2)*. |
| Schedule of Documents Relied Upon *(family only)* | Three-row Q&A. Mandated by *FCA 1964 §7(2)*. |

### Section conventions per case type

| Template | Includes |
|---|---|
| `civil_declaration` | Plaint + Verification + Vakalatnama only. CPC does not strictly require schedule pages at filing; supporting documents are produced under *Order VII Rules 14–18 CPC* during the framing of issues. |
| `civil_recovery_of_money`, `civil_permanent_injunction`, `civil_specific_performance`, `civil_possession_of_property` | Plaint + Verification + Schedule of Witnesses + Schedule of Documents Produced + Vakalatnama. Schedules are not statutorily required for ordinary civil suits but are filed in practice in Punjab district courts and follow Mulla's recommended form. |
| All 5 family templates (`family_khula`, `family_maintenance`, `family_dowry_recovery`, `family_minor_custody`, `family_conjugal_rights`) | Plaint + Verification + Schedule of Witnesses + Schedule of Documents Produced + Schedule of Documents Relied Upon + Vakalatnama. All three schedule pages are mandated by *Family Courts Act 1964 §7(2)*. |

## Digital signature placeholders

Every signing point in every template carries an explicit marker:

```
____________________________________
[DIGITAL SIGNATURE — PLAINTIFF]
Name: {{plaintiff_name}}
Date: {{signature_date}}
```

This appears (a) after the PRAYER block on the plaint, (b) at the end of the Verification page, and (c) at the foot of each Schedule page. The marker reserves a clear anchor for digital signatures when Module 4 (Digital Signatures & Audit Trail) lands. For now, the lawyer prints and hand-signs at the marked locations.

## Common to all four templates

### Pleading-structure authorities (universal)

| Authority | Provision | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| Civil Procedure Code, 1908 (Act V of 1908) | **Order VII Rule 1** | Particulars in plaint: court name, plaintiff + defendant descriptions, cause of action, jurisdiction, relief, valuation. Drives every paragraph block. |
| Civil Procedure Code, 1908 | **Order VI Rule 15** | Verification of pleadings. Language of the Verification page is taken verbatim. |
| Civil Procedure Code, 1908 | **Order VI Rule 2** | Pleading material facts only, not evidence. Drives the `[FACT NARRATIVE]` placeholder pattern. |
| Court Fees Act, 1870 | Schedule II Art. 17(iii) for declaratory suits; general for others | "Requisite court fee has been affixed" paragraph in every template. |
| Suits Valuation Act, 1887 | Section 4 | Cited in civil declaration template for valuation. |

### Pakistani drafting authorities (textbooks)

These are the case-drafting authorities cited inline in each `build*Document()` function in the generator. They are the recognised reference works in Pakistani legal academia and practice:

- **D.F. Mulla**, *The Code of Civil Procedure* — universal CPC commentary. Plaint structure follows Mulla's recommended form for civil suits.
- **Justice Tanzil-ur-Rahman**, *A Code of Muslim Personal Law*, Vol. I (Family Suits) & Vol. II (Maintenance, Dowry, Stridhan) — Forms K-1 (Khula), M-1 (Maintenance), D-1 (Dowry / Stridhan recovery).
- **Aamer Raza A. Khan**, *Drafting, Pleadings & Conveyancing* — Form D-3 (declaration suits against public authorities); model for civil declaration template.
- **Manzoor Hussain Sial**, *Drafting & Pleadings* (practitioner's manual) — cross-referenced for verification and supporting-document page conventions.
- **Mulla (Hidayatullah ed.)**, *Principles of Mahomedan Law* — Islamic family-law underpinnings of Khula, maintenance, and dowry.

## Template 1 — `family/family_khula.docx`

**Subject:** Suit for Dissolution of Marriage on the basis of Khula

| # | Authority | Provision | Used at |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dissolution of Muslim Marriages Act, 1939 (Act VIII of 1939) | **Section 2** — grounds for dissolution | Implicit basis; Khula itself is judicially recognised relief rather than codified in DMMA. |
| 2 | *Khurshid Bibi v Muhammad Amin* | **PLD 1967 SC 97** | Landmark SC judgment establishing Khula as the wife's right when marriage within "limits of Allah" has become impossible. Language quoted in para 10. |
| 3 | *Mst. Bilqis Fatima v Najmul Ikram* | PLD 1959 (W.P.) Lah 566 | Earlier authority recognising Khula on judicial decree. |
| 4 | Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 (Ord. VIII of 1961) | **Section 5** — Nikah registration | Para 2: "marriage duly registered in accordance with Section 5 of MFLO". |
| 5 | Family Courts Act, 1964 (Act XXXV of 1964) | **Section 5** — jurisdiction of Family Courts over Schedule matters | Para 13: jurisdiction clause. |
| 6 | Family Courts Act, 1964 | **Section 7(2)** — plaint shall include Schedule of witnesses + documents | Drives the three "Schedule" pages. |
| 7 | Civil Procedure Code, 1908 | **Order VII Rule 1** | Particulars in plaint. |

**Drafting form:** *Tanzil-ur-Rahman, Vol. I, Form K-1 (Khula).*

**Jurisprudential basis (Islamic):**
- *Quran 2:229* — basis for Khula (woman's right to seek dissolution on returning Mahr).
- Hadith of *Jamila bint Abdullah* (Sahih Bukhari) — first reported case of Khula in Islamic history.

## How the templates flow through LawFlow (recap)

1. **Lawyer creates case** → picks Civil/Family → picks case type (one of the 10 seeded in `case_types`) → fills client/opposite-party form. *(Module 3 Phase 1 — landed on `feat/module-3-document-editor`.)*
2. **Editor opens** → backend resolves `case_type.code` → loads `case-templates/<category>/<code>.docx`. *(Module 3 Phase 2 — pending.)*
3. **Mammoth converts `.docx` → HTML** → Tiptap renders the editable plaint; sidebar reads `<h1>` section headings for navigation.
4. **Lawyer fills `{{placeholders}}`** by typing over them or, in the structured-field UI, via form inputs that auto-substitute.
5. **Lawyer adds supporting documents** (Module 3 Phase 3 — pending).
6. **System compiles plaint + attachments into a single PDF** for registrar submission (Module 3 Phase 4 — pending, satisfies Functional Requirement FE-8).

# Bibliography & How to Access the Sources

The references cited above fall into three tiers, in descending order of authoritative weight (and ascending order of how easy they are to lose marks if you can't produce them in viva):

1. **Primary statutes** — the Acts and Ordinances themselves. Always trump anything else. **All free online.**
2. **Case law (PLD / SCMR / YLR / etc.)** — Pakistani court judgments. Free or low-cost online.
3. **Textbooks (drafting authorities)** — commentary by scholars and judges. Mostly print, but the standard ones are available in any Bar Council or law-school library.

If a teacher questions the templates, point to **Tier 1 + Tier 2** first — these are objective and verifiable. Textbooks are supporting authority, not gospel.

---

Pakistani case citations use this format: *Case Name*, **YEAR** *Report* **Volume/Page**. Examples:

| Citation | Court | Subject | Where to read |

- 🌐 **supremecourt.gov.pk/case-laws** — search for case name or party; free.

- 📕 **Hard-copy** — every Bar Council library and major law-school library keeps bound PLD volumes back to the 1950s.

## Citation format for the FYP report

Pakistani legal academia mostly follows a hybrid of OSCOLA (Oxford Standard Citation of Legal Authorities) and PLD's own convention. Acceptable styles for the report:

**Statutes:**
> *Family Courts Act 1964*, s. 7(2).
> *Specific Relief Act 1877*, ss. 42 and 55.

**Case law:**
> *Khurshid Bibi v Muhammad Amin*, PLD 1967 SC 97.
> *Hafiz Tassaduq Hussain v Muhammad Din*, PLD 2011 SC 241.

**Textbooks:**
> Mulla, *The Code of Civil Procedure*, 18th edn. (LexisNexis 2017), Order VII Rule 1.
> Mulla, *Principles of Mahomedan Law* (Hidayatullah edn., LexisNexis 2017), §285.
> Tanzil-ur-Rahman, *A Code of Muslim Personal Law*, Vol I, Form K-1.

**Quranic verses:**
> Quran 2:229 (basis for Khula).
> Quran 2:233 and 65:6 (husband's maintenance obligation).
